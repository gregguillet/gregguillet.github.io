# Research

## Glacier Dynamics and Surge-Type Glaciers

My work on glacier dynamics focuses on understanding the mechanisms behind fast ice flow and glacier surges. I have developed comprehensive inventories of surge-type glaciers in High Mountain Asia and globally, using multi-factor remote sensing approaches combining satellite velocity, elevation change, and SAR backscatter data.

## Bayesian Methods and Uncertainty Quantification

A central theme in my research is the development of Bayesian frameworks for quantifying uncertainty in cryospheric observations and models. This includes methods for estimating glacier surface elevation changes from digital elevation models, as well as probabilistic approaches to camera calibration and monoplotting for historical photograph analysis.

## Data Assimilation for the Cryosphere

I develop ensemble-based data assimilation methods to fuse uncertain models with noisy satellite observations. Current applications include constraining global snow mass estimates, calibrating glacier mass balance projections, and producing cryospheric reanalyses that bridge observational gaps.

## Snow Science and Natural Hazards

My applied work addresses cryospheric natural hazards, including snow avalanche modeling and the study of ice aprons on steep alpine rock faces — features whose degradation poses growing risks in high-mountain environments such as the Mont-Blanc massif.
